What's this dog?
The French Bulldog, otherwise known as the "Frenchie," is a small dog breed originally bred in the 1800s to be small versions of the Bulldog, typically seen as companions of lace workers in England then transitioning to be lap dogs of high-society women.

These dogs can thrive in a multitude of living spaces from small apartments to large homes, love to stick by their humans at all times, and are known to be low on barking scale. While Frenchies do get cases of "zoomies" and love to go on walks they are also happy cuddling you while you're on the couch watching TV.

These dogs can tire easily meaning a short walk a day is capable of satisfying them, but Frenchies are very heat sensitive meaning you may need to stick to the shade or even use a treadmill indoors if it's too hot outside.
